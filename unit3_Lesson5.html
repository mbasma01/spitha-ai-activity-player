Lesson 05: Data Anatomy

Features & Targets

Mastering the art of identifying the clues and the answers in any dataset.

The "What" and the "Why"

In Machine Learning, we don't just dump a bucket of data into a computer. We organize it so the computer can learn a specific relationship.

We call the Inputs "Features" ($X$) and the Output the "Target" ($y$). To a mathematician, this is $y = f(X)$. To a Data Scientist, it's the foundation of every prediction.

Variable: X

Features

"The Clues"

Variable: y

Target

"The Answer"

🕵️‍♂️

The Detective Analogy

The fingerprints, the timestamp, and the witness statements are Features. The identity of the culprit is the Target.

⛈️

The Weather Analogy

The air pressure and wind speed are the Features. Whether it will rain tomorrow is the Target.

Exercise 2: The Goal Switch

In ML, the Target depends entirely on the question you are asking. The same column can be a feature in one model and a target in another.

Model A Goal: Determine if a customer will click a "Renew Subscription" button.

Model B Goal: Estimate how much a customer is willing to pay per month.

Exactly. The "Answer" (Target) is always the thing you are trying to predict!
