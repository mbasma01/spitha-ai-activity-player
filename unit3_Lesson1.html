AI Literacy: What is AI?

Unlocking the mystery of how machines transition from following "recipes" to discovering "patterns."

The Paradigm Shift

For 50 years, computers were Rule-Based. A human expert wrote every "If-Then" statement. If a computer saw an email, it looked for the word "WINNER" and marked it spam. This was rigid and easy to trick.

Modern AI is "Learning-Based." Instead of rules, we give it a Neural Network—a mathematical structure inspired by the human brain. We don't tell it what "spam" looks like; we show it 10 million spam emails and let it notice the subtle, complex patterns itself.

The Two Paths of Learning

AI models aren't all trained the same way. Which path does the data take?

"The Answer Key"

Supervised Learning

The computer learns with a teacher. We provide the data and the answers (labels).

Q: "Is this photo a cat?"
A: "Yes, this is a cat." (The Label)
"Self-Discovery"

Unsupervised Learning

The computer learns on its own. We provide data, but no answers. It finds hidden groupings.

Q: "What do these 1 million users have in common?"
A: "I found 3 clusters: Gamers, Cooks, and Hikers."

Understanding through Analogy

A

The Strict Clerk (Rules)

Imagine a clerk who only accepts forms signed in black ink. If you bring a blue ink form, they reject it immediately. They have no common sense—only a manual of rules. This is how old software works.

B

The Toddler (AI)

Imagine a toddler watching 1,000 people open a door. They notice that people grab the handle and pull. Nobody gave the toddler a manual. They observed the patterns of success and learned on their own. This is AI.
