Checkpoint: Data Cleaning

Clean data is the foundation of visualization. If your data is dirty, your charts will lie. Review the core concepts below before proving your mastery.

Concept Study Guide

The Void (Missing Data)

Scenario: Empty cells, NaN, or null values.

The Risk: Machine Learning models (like Scikit-Learn) often crash instantly. Visualizations will show gaps or drop entire categories.

# If <5% missing: Drop
df.dropna(inplace=True)
# If >5% missing: Impute
df.fillna(df.mean(), inplace=True)

The Impossible (Outliers)

Scenario: Values that defy logic (Age: 250) or statistics (Income: $1B in a local survey).

The Risk: Drastically skews the Mean. A single billionaire makes a poor village look rich on average.

# Filter logical range
df = df[(df['age'] < 120) & (df['age'] > 0)]

The Clones (Duplicates)

Scenario: Identical rows repeated due to system glitches or double-entry.

The Risk: Inflates metrics (double revenue) and introduces bias (over-weighting specific events).

# Remove exact matches
df.drop_duplicates(inplace=True)

The Mess (Inconsistency)

Scenario: Variations like "NY", "New York", "ny", "N.Y.".

The Risk: Grouping fails. You get 4 small bars instead of 1 big bar for New York sales.

# Map to standard
df.replace({'ny': 'New York'}, inplace=True)

The Imposter (Data Types)

Scenario: Numbers stored as text (e.g., "$100" or "1,000").

The Risk: Math acts like text concatenation. "1"+"1"="11" instead of 2.

# Clean & Convert
df['price'] = df['price'].str.replace('$','').astype(float)

The Phantom (Whitespace)

Scenario: Invisible spaces: "Apple" vs "Apple ".

The Risk: Filtering for df['fruit'] == 'Apple' misses half your data.

# Strip leading/trailing space
df['col'] = df['col'].str.strip()
