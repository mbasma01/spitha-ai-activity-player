The "Ordered Data" Trap ⚠️

Datasets like Iris are stored perfectly sorted. Look at what happens if you take the bottom 20% without shuffling.

Iris Dataset (Sorted)

Setosa (Rows 0-49)
Versicolor (Rows 50-99)
Virginica (Rows 100-149)

The Test Set will ONLY see Virginica!

NO Shuffle Split:

Model trains on species A and B. It is tested on species C. The AI has never seen a 'C' flower.
Result: 0% Test Accuracy.

Shuffled Split:

Data is mixed like a deck of cards. Both Train and Test sets get a healthy mix of A, B, and C.
Result: Fair Science.

What happens when the AI doesn't have enough "Evidence" to find the pattern?

Large Training Sample

Scenario 4

The Data Leakage Trap

"My model has 100% accuracy, but it failed in the real world. Why?"

The Contamination

You accidentally included the answer (the Target) as a feature, or you calculated averages using the test set rows before splitting. The model "cheated" by seeing the future.

The Solution

Always split your data as the very first step. Treat the test set as a locked vault until the final evaluation.
