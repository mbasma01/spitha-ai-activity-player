Supervised Learning

How we train machines using "Labeled Data" to make highly accurate predictions about the future.

The Guided Approach

Think of a student preparing for a test with an answer key. For every practice problem (the Data), the student can check the back of the book (the Label) to see if they got it right.

In AI, this "Answer Key" is called Labeled Data. By seeing millions of pairs of questions and answers, the model learns the mathematical relationship between them.

Crucial Rule:

Supervised learning requires Historical Data. You cannot predict the price of a house if you've never seen what other houses sold for in the past.

The Two Branches

How we choose to categorize or calculate our results.

1. Classification

The goal is to put things into Categories or "buckets." It's discrete logic: Is it this group or that group?

Use Case: Medical Imaging
Output: "Healthy" vs. "Cancerous"

2. Regression

The goal is to predict a Continuous Number along a range. It answers "How Much?" or "How Many?"

Use Case: Stock Market
Output: Target Price (e.g., $152.40)
